Strength Training Philosophy

Muscle definition + injury prevention

Phase 1–2 Focus (Full Body)

  • Squats & Deadlifts — posterior chain for running power
  • Push-ups / Chest Press — upper body baseline
  • Rows & Pull-ups — back strength, posture
  • Lunges & Step-ups — running specific legs
  • Core work — planks, dead bugs, Russian twists daily
  • 3 sets × 10–12 reps, progressive overload each week

Phase 3–4 Focus (Maintenance)

  • Running takes priority — volume of strength drops
  • Bodyweight + light weights to retain muscle
  • Heavy on single-leg work — glute bridges, step-ups
  • Injury prevention — calf raises, hip flexor work
  • Friday is always run/tempo — never strength on Friday
  • Saturday is always active recovery — never training
  • Never strength train the day before a long run — Saturday is sacred rest
Weekly structure (all phases): Mon — Strength | Tue — Run | Wed — REST | Thu — Strength | Fri — Run/Tempo | Sat — Active Recovery | Sun — Long Run. Two rest/recovery days every single week. This is not optional — it's where adaptation happens.

Eating Strategy

Pre-Run Nutrition

  • Easy runs (<45 min): Fasted or light snack — banana, black coffee
  • Long runs (60+ min): Light meal 90 min before — oats, banana, peanut butter
  • During long runs (>90 min): 30–60g carbs/hour — dates, gels, raisins
  • Always hydrate 500ml 1hr before

Post-Run Recovery Window

  • Eat within 45 minutes post-run
  • Protein + Carbs combo — 3:1 carb-to-protein ratio
  • Options: Eggs + rice, paneer + roti, whey + banana shake
  • Avoid high-fat in this window — slows absorption

Indian Meal Planning — What Works

  • Breakfast: 4 eggs (2 whole + 2 whites) + 2 whole wheat rotis + black coffee
  • Lunch: 200g paneer/chicken + 1 cup dal + 1 cup sabji + 2 rotis or 1 cup rice
  • Pre-workout snack: Greek yogurt + banana OR handful of mixed nuts + dates
  • Post-workout: Whey shake (30g protein) + 1 banana OR egg whites + quick carb
  • Dinner: Large salad + 200g lean protein (chicken/fish/paneer) + vegetables
  • Avoid: Maida, fried snacks, packaged food, excessive chai with sugar, alcohol on training days
  • Cheat meal: 1x per week — not a cheat day. Controlled indulgence, not a binge.

Supplements Worth Taking

The Short List (Evidence-Based Only)

  • Whey Protein: Only if you can't hit 180g protein from food. Not magic, just convenient.
  • Creatine Monohydrate: 5g/day, every day. Retains muscle during fat loss. Cheap and proven.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2: Most Indians are deficient. Supports muscle function and immunity.
  • Magnesium Glycinate: 300mg at night. Aids sleep quality and muscle recovery.
  • Electrolytes: On long run days — coconut water, ORS, or electrolyte tabs. Not during short runs.
  • Skip: Fat burners, BCAAs (redundant with sufficient protein), expensive pre-workouts.
